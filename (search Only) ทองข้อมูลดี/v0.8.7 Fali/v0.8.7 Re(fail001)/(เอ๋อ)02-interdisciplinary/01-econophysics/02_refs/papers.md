# Econophysics Literature References

## Foundational Papers

### 1. Mandelbrot (1963)
**Title:** The Variation of Certain Speculative Prices  
**Journal:** Journal of Business  
**Key finding:** Fat tails in price distributions  
**Relevance:** First evidence of power-law behavior in markets

---

### 2. Gopikrishnan et al. (1999)
**Title:** Scaling of the distribution of fluctuations of financial market indices  
**Journal:** Physical Review E  
**Key finding:** α ≈ 3 (inverse cubic law)  
**Relevance:** Empirical confirmation of universal exponent

---

### 3. Stanley et al. (1999)
**Title:** Scaling and Universality in Economics  
**Journal:** Physica A  
**Key finding:** Econophysics methodology  
**Relevance:** Foundational econophysics framework

---

### 4. Mantegna & Stanley (2000)
**Title:** An Introduction to Econophysics  
**Publisher:** Cambridge University Press  
**Key finding:** Textbook on econophysics  
**Relevance:** Comprehensive methodology

---

### 5. Bouchaud & Potters (2003)
**Title:** Theory of Financial Risk and Derivative Pricing  
**Publisher:** Cambridge University Press  
**Key finding:** Statistical physics of risk  
**Relevance:** Theoretical framework

---

## Mean Reversion Literature

### 6. Poterba & Summers (1988)
**Title:** Mean Reversion in Stock Prices  
**Journal:** Journal of Financial Economics  
**Key finding:** Evidence for mean reversion  
**Relevance:** F = -∇Ω interpretation

---

### 7. Fama & French (1988)
**Title:** Permanent and Temporary Components of Stock Prices  
**Journal:** Journal of Political Economy  
**Key finding:** Predictable variation  
**Relevance:** Supports potential-based model

---

## Power Law Literature

### 8. Gabaix et al. (2003)
**Title:** A Theory of Power-Law Distributions

**Key finding:** Mechanism for α ≈ 3

---

### 9. Plerou et al. (2000)
**Title:** Economic fluctuations and diffusion  
**Journal:** Physical Review E  
**Key finding:** Anomalous diffusion  
**Relevance:** Connection to physics

---

### 10. Lux & Marchesi (1999)
**Title:** Scaling and criticality in a stochastic multi-agent model  
**Journal:** Nature  
**Key finding:** Agent-based explanation  
**Relevance:** Microscopic foundation
