## 1. Introduction: The Price of Existence
Common sense tells us that "Being" is free. We just *are*.
Thermodynamics tells us the opposite: **"Being" costs Energy.**
To exist, to move, to interact requires a transaction.
The fundamental rule of our reality is simple: **You cannot spend what you do not have.**

## 2. The Speed Limit: Why Instant Travel is Impossible
Imagine you want to travel to a star 10 light-years away.
*   **Intuition**: "I want to be there NOW."
*   **Reality**: To be there *now* (instantly) would require infinite energy.
*   **The Limit ($c$)**: The "Speed of Light" is not an arbitrary number we put into the equations. **It emerged naturally from the calculations.** When we balanced the energy cost of distance against the energy cost of time, the math produced a specific ratio: $299,792,458$ m/s. It is the **Maximum Processing Rate** of the Universe.
*   **Conclusion**: Distance is simply a measure of the **Cost** (in Time/Energy) required to update your position. $c$ is the exchange rate.

## 3. Relativity: The Lag of Motion
Einstein discovered that "Time slows down" when you move fast. This breaks common sense.
But let's look at it through **Cost**:
*   You have a fixed budget of "Processing Power" ($c$).
*   If you spend 99% of your budget on **Motion** (moving through space).
*   You only have 1% left for **Processing** (existence/time).
*   **Result**: Your internal clock slows down. Not because of magic, but because you are running out of bandwidth.
*   **Analogy**: It is exactly like **Server Lag**. When a player moves too fast, the server struggles to update their position, and they "lag" relative to everyone else.

## 4. The Past Star: Seeing What Was
When we look at a star, we see the past. Why?
*   Because the information had to "pay the toll" to travel to us.
*   If we teleported there instantly, we would see the future (relative to our view).
*   **The Paradox**: "Is the star really there?"
*   **The Answer**: Yes. But our connection to it is limited by the **Latency** of the universe. We are always interacting with the "Receipt" of the star, not the Star itself.
